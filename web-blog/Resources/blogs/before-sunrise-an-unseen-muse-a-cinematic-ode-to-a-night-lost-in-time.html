Before Sunrise: An Unseen Muse, A Cinematic Ode to a Night Lost in Time

Back in 1989, Richard Linklater, then an unknown person in his late 20s, was passing through a toy store at night in Philadephia when he met a women. They started having an exalarating conversation about life, art, films, people. At one point of their conversation, Rich stopped and told her, “You know, I’m gonna make a movie about this.” She asked him, “About what?” He replied, “This… This conversation.” They eventually ended up spending the night walking & talking. Though initially they stayed in touch over the telephone, they lost contact eventually.

And he did made a movie about this.

The Before Trilogy as we know of today, consists of three movies with a consequent gap of 9 years Before Sunrise (1995), Before Sunset (2004), Before Midnight (2013), was actually about Richard’s past experience with someone he spent time with. The first movie is about Jesse and Celine who met in a train and decided to stop in Vienna and spend a day talking to each other and the second and third follows these two characters meeting again after ages and their conversation with each other, in a different life, in a different time.

In 1994, Linklater started shooting “Before Sunrise” and it eventually turned out to be one of the best films in his life. When the world premiere was about to take place, Linklater was secretly hoping that the women he met that night by the toy store would show up because she’d know that this was the movie about her, but she did not. 10 years later, Linklater shot the sequel Before Sunset (2004) and had yet to hear from her. She didn’t show up. Linklater was hoping that life, would imitate art in this case, just like how Jesse and Celine met after 9 years of losing contact. But it didn’t. He would often think, “Okay I’ve got a screening in Philadephia, maybe she’ll show up.”

She never did.

Finally, in May 2010, Linklater got from a friend of the women, and learned the reason why she didn’t show up. 16 years ago from that day she died in a motorcycle accident at the age of 24 (on May 9, 1994), just a few weeks before he started shooting Before Sunrise (1995). She didn’t live to see a masterpiece she made with just one night conversation with some person she met in a toy store. In 2013, when the last installment of the Before Triology was released, Linklater dedicated the film to the women who inspired him. I often wonder what’d have happened if Linklater learned she died just before the shooting of the first movie. Maybe we wouldn’t got jesse or Celine, let alone the trilogy.

Dedicated to the memory of Amy Lehrhaupt, the woman who was the inspiration for Before Sunrise (1995), and later The Before Trilogy.
